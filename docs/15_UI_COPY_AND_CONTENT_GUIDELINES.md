# WashGo — UI Copy & Content Guidelines

Version: 1.0

Last Updated: July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the writing standards, terminology, tone of voice, and microcopy used throughout the WashGo platform.

It ensures that every button, message, notification, onboarding screen, dialog, and error message provides a consistent and user-friendly experience.

This document serves as the official writing guide for all developers, designers, and future contributors.

---

# Brand Voice

WashGo should sound:

Professional

Friendly

Helpful

Modern

Trustworthy

Simple

Confident

Never robotic.

Never overly technical.

Never use complicated words when simple words work better.

---

# Writing Principles

Always write:

Clear

Short

Action-oriented

Positive

Human

Avoid unnecessary technical language.

---

# Preferred Language Style

Good

"Your rider is on the way."

Bad

"The assigned delivery personnel is currently en route."

---

Good

"Pickup scheduled."

Bad

"Pickup scheduling operation completed successfully."

# Error Messages

Always explain what happened and what the user can do.

Good

"We couldn't connect to the internet. Please try again."

Bad

"Network Error."

---

Good

"Unable to load nearby laundries."

Button

Retry

---

# Confirmation Dialogs

# Tone During Problems

Never blame the user.

Instead of

"You entered the wrong password."

Use

"The password doesn't match our records."

---

Instead of

"You failed to upload your photo."

Use

"We couldn't upload your photo. Please try again."

---

# Accessibility Writing

Avoid abbreviations.

Avoid jargon.

Use simple words.

Support screen readers.

Keep sentences concise.

---

# Future Localization

All text should be prepared for:

English

Khmer

Avoid hardcoded strings.

Every user-facing text should be stored in localization files.
